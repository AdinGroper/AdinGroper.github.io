Nîmes

March 2024

Nîmes is...

After not having stayed in Toulouse for a couple weekends in a row, I knew I had to get out and explore more of France during my semester abroad. When I found out my friends Hubert, Alvin, and Shaofeng were planning a trip to Nimes this particular weekend, I decided to tag along.

The trip to Nimes was only a few hours by train from Toulouse. We left campus Saturday morning and arrived in Nimes later that afternoon. Taking the train anywhere in the south of France was pretty convenient and was not too expensive either - definitely something I appreciated living in Toulouse.

Shortly after arriving in Nimes, we got ourselves local transporation cards. The plan was to take public transporation to see the Pont-du-Gard, an aqueduct built by the Romans that still stands today just northeast of Nimes. Following a quick lunch, we headed to the bus station and hopped on one that would take us to the Pont-du-Gard. However, after taking the buses as far as we could, passing through small towns and neighborhoods in the countryside, we were eventually dropped off at small bus stop next to the highway. Stranded, we managed to order an Uber to take us the rest of the way.

Our Uber driver was quite surprised to find us standing in what was probably the middle of nowhere. And because we were a captive audience, he figured he'd charge us extra for having a fourth person in the car. We were kind of annoyed by this, but we didn't really have a choice. The Uber driver ended up being hilarious too, speaking to us in broken English and playing the air guitar on our drive over. Eventually we arrived at the Pont-du-Gard and he gave us his business card in case we wanted a ride back, which was nice.

When we finally approached the aqueduct, it was super cool to see in person. The Pont-du-Gard, which translates to "Bridge of the Gard", was originally constructed around the 1st century AD by the Romans to transport water to the city of Nimes. At the time, Nimes was an important outpost in this region for the Roman Empire. The Pont-du-Gard is one of several feats of Roman engineering in and around Nimes that is still standing today.
